# EMOVEL Instagram System

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how EMOVEL should use Instagram as a premium commercial content channel.

Its purpose is to help EMOVEL AI create Instagram content for:

- premium digital products
- offers
- campaigns
- prompt packs
- brand authority
- product launches
- client deliverables
- founder positioning
- product education

Instagram content should not be treated as casual posting. It should be part of the EMOVEL commercial system: a channel for authority, education, product clarity, and controlled conversion.

The goal is not virality. The goal is qualified attention.

## 2. Instagram Role Inside EMOVEL

Instagram is a visibility and trust layer.

Inside EMOVEL, Instagram should:

- clarify the brand's point of view
- educate the audience
- show product logic
- build founder authority
- make offers easier to understand
- support launches
- turn ideas into market-facing assets
- create demand without cheap engagement tactics

Instagram should not carry the entire business.

It supports:

- landing pages
- shop assets
- prompt packs
- product launches
- campaign systems
- client deliverables
- email and export flows

The role of Instagram is to make the market more ready before the offer is presented.

## 3. Authority Positioning

EMOVEL authority is built through clarity, structure, and commercial judgment.

Authority content should show:

- how EMOVEL thinks
- what EMOVEL values
- what EMOVEL rejects
- how products are structured
- why generic AI output is not enough
- how offers, pages, prompts, and campaigns become monetizable assets

Authority should feel calm, not performative.

EMOVEL should not chase credibility through exaggerated claims. It should demonstrate credibility through useful frameworks, precise language, strong examples, and commercial restraint.

Authority positioning themes:

- controlled execution
- premium product architecture
- digital asset monetization
- offer clarity
- prompt system design
- structured campaigns
- Builder logic
- export-ready deliverables
- luxury-tech identity

The voice should feel like a senior operator explaining what matters.

## 5. Hook Rules

Hooks should be sharp, useful, and commercially relevant.

EMOVEL hooks should not rely on shock, fake controversy, or cheap curiosity.

Good hooks:

- name a real problem
- challenge a weak assumption
- reveal a commercial principle
- clarify a common mistake
- introduce a useful framework
- create immediate relevance for the right buyer

Hook patterns:

- "Most digital products do not fail because the idea is weak."
- "A prompt pack is not a list of prompts."
- "Your landing page does not need more sections. It needs clearer roles."
- "Generic AI output is fast. Structured output is reusable."
- "Before you launch the campaign, build the offer architecture."

Avoid:

- "You will not believe this."
- "This one secret changed everything."
- "Stop scrolling."
- "Nobody is talking about this."
- "The algorithm loves this."
- "I made six figures with this."

The hook should qualify the audience, not attract everyone.

## 6. Carousel Structure

Carousels should teach one idea clearly.

They should feel editorial, not crowded.

Recommended structure:

1. Cover slide: sharp premise
2. Context: why the idea matters
3. Problem: what is commonly broken
4. Framework: how to think about it
5. Breakdown: practical points
6. Example: before/after or pattern
7. Application: how the viewer can use it
8. CTA: calm next step

Carousel rules:

- One concept per carousel.
- One primary message per slide.
- Keep text concise.
- Use premium hierarchy.
- Avoid decorative filler.
- Avoid generic motivational slides.
- Use examples when possible.
- End with a useful CTA.

Good carousel topics:

- "The anatomy of a premium offer"
- "Why prompt packs need architecture"
- "How to structure a digital product page"
- "From idea to product system"
- "The difference between content and commercial assets"

Carousels should make the viewer smarter and closer to action.

## 7. Reel Structure

Reels should be concise, controlled, and useful.

They should not imitate noisy creator trends.

Recommended structure:

1. Opening line: clear commercial observation
2. Context: what the viewer is getting wrong
3. Framework: the EMOVEL way to think about it
4. Example: concrete application
5. Close: direct next step or principle

Reel formats:

- founder talking point
- screen walkthrough
- product logic explanation
- before/after critique
- framework breakdown
- launch note
- offer teardown

Reel rules:

- Keep the message focused.
- Avoid exaggerated facial performance.
- Avoid trend dependence.
- Avoid fake urgency.
- Use captions for clarity.
- Keep visual style premium and restrained.
- Prioritize authority over entertainment.

Reels should feel like concise editorial insight, not performance content.

## 8. Caption Structure

Captions should extend the post with clarity and commercial depth.

Recommended structure:

1. Opening statement
2. Explanation
3. Structured insight
4. Practical application
5. Calm CTA

Example structure:

Opening:

- "A digital product is not ready because the file is finished."

Explanation:

- "It is ready when the buyer can understand the problem, the outcome, the mechanism, the deliverables, and the next step."

Structured insight:

- "The product needs commercial architecture before it needs more content."

Practical application:

- "Before publishing, check the audience, value stack, pricing logic, and delivery format."

CTA:

- "Build the structure before scaling the launch."

Caption rules:

- Use short paragraphs.
- Keep line breaks intentional.
- Avoid excessive spacing.
- Avoid generic "save this" language.
- Avoid engagement bait.
- Do not overuse hashtags.
- Keep CTA aligned with the post.

## 9. CTA Rules

Instagram CTAs should be calm and specific.

CTA types:

- read the page
- enter the Builder
- build the offer
- download the asset
- review the framework
- upgrade when ready
- send a serious inquiry
- use the checklist

Good CTAs:

- "Build the offer before scaling the campaign."
- "Use this as a checklist before publishing."
- "Start with the Builder when the offer is clear."
- "Turn this into a structured product page."
- "Review the product architecture before launch."

Avoid:

- "Comment YES."
- "DM me the word RICH."
- "Claim this before it disappears."
- "Smash the link."
- "Do this now or stay stuck."

CTA should never lower the perceived value of EMOVEL.

It should guide serious action.

## 10. Launch Content System

Launch content should move the audience from awareness to decision.

Launch phases:

1. Context
2. Problem
3. Framework
4. Product education
5. Offer reveal
6. Objection handling
7. Use case proof
8. CTA
9. Final reminder
10. Post-launch orientation

Launch content should include:

- why the product exists
- who it is for
- what problem it solves
- what is included
- how it works
- how to use it
- why it is priced the way it is
- what to do next

Launch rules:

- Do not rely on countdown pressure.
- Do not fake scarcity.
- Do not exaggerate outcomes.
- Repeat the value from different angles.
- Keep the product visible.
- Make the CTA clear.

Launch content should feel like a premium product release, not a hype cycle.

Product education explains what EMOVEL does and why it matters.

It should reduce confusion and increase buyer confidence.

Product education topics:

- what the Core Orchestrator does
- how the Prompt Engine works
- what Builder outputs contain
- why Markdown/TXT/PDF exports matter
- how Pro unlocks private PDF export
- how prompt systems differ from prompt lists
- how a campaign pack is structured
- how an offer becomes a landing page

Product education structure:

1. Name the feature or product.
2. Explain the problem it solves.
3. Show the mechanism.
4. Clarify the output.
5. Explain the use case.
6. Add a direct CTA.

Product education should feel practical and premium.

Avoid feature dumping. Always connect features to commercial use.

## 13. Mistakes To Avoid

Avoid:

- generic influencer advice
- motivational filler
- viral hacks
- cheap engagement bait
- guru language
- fake urgency
- exaggerated claims
- fake income screenshots
- algorithm obsession
- overposting without strategy
- trendy hooks that damage premium positioning
- vague brand inspiration
- carousel clutter
- Reels that feel childish
- captions with no commercial point
- CTAs that beg for interaction

Also avoid:

- using Instagram as the whole funnel
- posting content disconnected from offers
- making every post educational with no product path
- making every post promotional with no authority
- confusing aesthetics with strategy

EMOVEL Instagram should be consistent, useful, and commercially sharp.

## 14. Instagram Quality Checklist

Before approving Instagram content, check:

- The post has one clear idea.
- The audience is specific.
- The hook is relevant, not cheap.
- The content connects to a commercial theme.
- The tone is calm and premium.
- The post avoids hype.
- The post avoids fake urgency.
- The CTA is clear and restrained.
- The content supports an offer, product, or authority position.
- The caption adds value.
- The format matches the idea.
- The post does not sound like generic AI output.
- The visual direction remains luxury-tech.
- The product path is clear when relevant.
- The post can attract qualified attention.

If the content could belong to any AI brand, rewrite it.

If the hook attracts the wrong audience, sharpen it.

If the post is useful but has no commercial connection, add context.
